⚖️ Why this version works better for 22 days
The Orléans → Blois stretch (cut from the full version) is the flattest and least remarkable part of the route — pleasant but not spectacular. Starting in Blois puts you immediately into the château heartland. You lose nothing of significance and gain a more focused, intense experience. The 4 rest days are placed at Tours (1 night), Saumur (1 night) and Nantes (2 nights) — the cities most worth lingering in.

Blois → Tours

Days 1–7 · ~65 km · Straight into château country. The Garden Festival, Leonardo da Vinci's home, and a 2-night city rest in Tours. The most enchanting stretch of the Loire.

Day 1 · 1 Aug — Bikes & Blois
🚲 Blois — Pick up bikes & explore
0 km
You arrived in Blois the evening before (31 Jul, midday train from Paris Austerlitz) and slept here. This morning, pick up bikes at Détours de Loire Blois. Spend the day on wheels around the city — the Royal Château is right in the centre. Evening: son-et-lumière light show on the château façade (runs July–August, brilliant for kids, ~€10). Set up camp and get an early night before the first riding day.
🏕️ Stay
Camping du Lac de Loire (~3 km from centre)
Or treat to 1 hotel night in Blois to start fresh.

Day 2 · 2 Aug
🌸 Blois → Chaumont-sur-Loire
~20 km
Short first riding day — everyone finds their rhythm. The path hugs the Loire through poplar groves. Chaumont's château hosts the International Garden Festival all summer: 30+ contemporary themed gardens installed on the grounds. Arrive by 10am and spend the whole day exploring. One of the most memorable stops for children on the entire route.
🏕️ Stay
Camping de la Grande Tortue, Candé-sur-Beuvron (~3 km from château). Pool, excellent family site.
+33 2 54 44 15 20
la-grande-tortue.com
🌸 Garden Festival
Runs Apr–Nov. ~30 themed contemporary gardens. Adult ~€20 / child 6–15 ~€10 / under 6 free.
domaine-chaumont.fr
📝 Tip
Leave Blois at 7:30am, arrive by 10am, full day for the gardens before the heat peaks. The château interior is also worth 45 minutes.

Day 3 · 3 Aug
🎨 Chaumont → Amboise
~23 km
One of the most beautiful stages on the route — willow-lined levées with constant river views. Amboise is the undisputed highlight of Phase 1. The Clos Lucé is Leonardo da Vinci's final home, with life-size models of his inventions (flying machine, tank, helicopter, swing bridge) scattered through the gardens. The 8 and 11-year-old will spend hours here. The Royal Château above town is also impressive.

Leonardo is buried here. Terrace views over the Loire are stunning. Adult ~€15 / child ~€10.

Day 4 · 4 Aug
🏊 Amboise — River rest day
0 km 😴
If you didn't fit Clos Lucé and the Château yesterday, this is your chance. Otherwise: the Loire has a sandy river beach just below the campsite, brilliant for young children all morning. Kayak hire available in town. The Friday/Sunday market is excellent. This is the first real "resort" feeling of the trip — savour it fully.
😴 Rest day🏊 River beach🛶 Kayaking
Day 5 · 5 Aug
🏛️ Amboise → Tours
~25 km
Follow the south bank through the Montlouis-sur-Loire vineyards — flat, shaded, beautiful. Tours is the biggest city on the route. Get an AirBnb for the night: washing machine, real kitchen, space to spread out after 5 days camping. The medieval old town (Place Plumereau, Rue Colbert) is extraordinary. Best restaurant city on the entire Loire.
🏠 Stay
AirBnb in Tours old town — 3+ bedrooms, washing machine. Budget €100–150/night. Book well ahead for August.
airbnb.com
🚲 Bike Service
Détours de Loire Tours (HQ, next to train station). Bring any issues here — they fix most things same-day.
37 Rue Charles Gille
🍽️ Tours Food
Rillettes de Tours, Tarte Tatin, Vouvray white wine. Place Plumereau and Rue Colbert have the best restaurants. Don't miss the covered market, Les Halles.

Day 6 · 6 Aug — Rest Day
🐠 Tours — City day
0 km 😴
Tours earns a full day. Morning: Touraine Aquarium (10,000 fish, underwater tunnels — excellent for the young ones). Afternoon: medieval cathedral, wander the old town. Practical: full laundry in the AirBnb, repack panniers, shed any excess weight, check all bikes at the Détours de Loire shop. Optional day trip to Château de Villandry (20 min by taxi) — the finest Renaissance gardens in France.

Day 7 · 7 Aug
🏰 Tours → Azay-le-Rideau
~28 km
Leave Tours and follow the Indre valley — quieter, more intimate, and beautifully shaded compared to the main Loire levée. Pass through Villandry if you skipped the day trip. Azay-le-Rideau: one of the most beautiful buildings in France — a Renaissance château floating on the Indre river, reflected perfectly in the water. The 4-year-old will call it the princess castle.
🏕️ Stay
Camping Municipal du Sabot, Azay-le-Rideau — 5 min walk from château. Simple but well-located. ~€12/night.
+33 2 47 45 42 72
🏰 Château d'Azay-le-Rideau
Built 1518, surrounded by a moat fed by the Indre. UNESCO World Heritage. Adult ~€13 / under 18 EU free. Summer evening light shows in the grounds.
azay-le-rideau.fr
📝 Route note
This is a slightly longer day at 28 km. The Indre valley is shadier than the Loire levée — better for August heat. Leave by 7am, Villandry by 9am, Azay by noon.
🏕️ Camp🏰 Floating château👸 Princess castle!

Tours → Saumur

Days 8–13 · ~80 km · Château country at its absolute richest. Sleeping Beauty, Richard the Lionheart, troglodyte caves, and an island campsite in the middle of the Loire.

🚲 5 riding days
😴 1 rest day (Saumur)

Day 8 · 8 Aug
👸 Azay → Château d'Ussé → Chinon
~25 km
Today has two unmissable châteaux. First: Ussé — the Sleeping Beauty castle. Gleaming white towers, turrets, formal gardens, wax tableaux of the fairy tale inside. The 4-year-old will be absolutely speechless. Then Chinon: a magnificent medieval clifftop city with one of France's largest château ruins and a fantastic riverside campsite on an island in the Vienne.
🏕️ Stay
Camping de l'Île Auger, Chinon — river island, excellent shaded pitches. ~€15–20/night.
+33 2 47 93 08 35
camping-chinon.com
👸 Château d'Ussé
The Sleeping Beauty castle. Wax fairy-tale scenes, peacocks in the gardens. Adult ~€17 / child 8–16 ~€11 / under 8 free.
chateaudusse.fr
⚔️ Forteresse Royale de Chinon
Clifftop fortress where Joan of Arc met Charles VII. Kids get a tablet for interactive VR tour. Adult ~€10 / child ~€6.
forteressechinon.fr
🏕️ Camp👸 Sleeping Beauty!🏰 2 châteaux in 1 day
Day 9 · 9 Aug
🕳️ Chinon — Troglodytes & caves
0 km 😴
Half rest, half explore. Chinon's troglodyte cave dwellings carved into the tufa cliff are utterly unique — some still lived in, many open for visits. The Caves Plouzeau wine cellar is a medieval cave carved into the rock (adults). River swimming below the campsite for the kids in the afternoon. The old town streets are lovely for a slow morning wander with pastries.
😴 Rest / easy day🕳️ Cave dwellings🏊 River swim🍷 Chinon wine caves
Day 10 · 10 Aug
⛪ Chinon → Fontevraud → Saumur
~30 km
The longest day of the trip — but packed with history to break it up. Stop at Fontevraud Abbey: one of Europe's largest medieval complexes, containing the tombs of Eleanor of Aquitaine and Richard the Lionheart. Then Saumur: the iconic white château on its promontory, and the best campsite on the entire route — on an island in the Loire.

Camping de l'Île d'Offard — on an island in the Loire. THE bucket-list campsite. 3-star, pool. August books out months ahead.

Tombs of Eleanor of Aquitaine & Richard the Lionheart. Best-preserved medieval abbey in France. Adult ~€12 / child ~€7.
fontevraud.fr
🏰 Château de Saumur
The iconic Loire château on its cliff. Great decorative arts museum inside. Mushroom caves nearby — fascinating underground world.

Day 11 · 11 Aug — Rest Day
🍄 Saumur — Island base
0 km 😴
Saumur is the best rest stop on the whole route. The Île d'Offard campsite feels like a resort — kids swim, you sit by the Loire. Must-do: the mushroom caves (Les Caves Louis de Grenelle) carved into the tufa cliff — completely cool in August heat, utterly unique, kids love the underground world. Sparkling Saumur wine tasting for the adults. Stock up for the next stretch.
🍄 Mushroom Caves
Les Caves Louis de Grenelle — 15°C underground, fascinating tour. Adult ~€10 / child ~€6. The most unusual attraction on the route.
louisdegrenelle.fr
🏊 Loire Island
The campsite is on the island — the kids can swim in the Loire shallows all morning. The view of Saumur château from the island is spectacular.
🥂 Wine Tasting
Saumur Mousseux (sparkling) is produced right here. The caves double as wine cellars. Several offer tastings. Ask at the campsite for the best nearby option.

Saumur → Angers

Days 12–16 · ~50 km · Gentle Anjou countryside. A Roman amphitheatre, France's only Loire island village, and a medieval giant fortress containing the world's largest medieval tapestry.

🚲 4 riding days
😴 1 rest day (Angers)
📍 3 overnight stops
~25 km avg
Day 12 · 12 Aug
🏟️ Saumur → Gennes → Les Rosiers
~25 km
A peaceful stage through lush Anjou countryside. Stop in Gennes — the small Roman amphitheatre here is free to enter and children can run around the ancient tiers where gladiators once fought. The landscape gets visibly greener as you enter Anjou. Les Rosiers is a quiet riverside village, good campsite, excellent boulangerie.
🏕️ Stay
Camping Val de Loire, Les Rosiers-sur-Loire — quiet, family-friendly, on the Loire. ~€14/night.
+33 2 41 51 94 33
🏟️ Amphithéâtre de Gennes
1st–2nd century Roman amphitheatre. Free entry. Not on the main route — ask locally or use Google Maps. 15-min detour, absolutely worth it. Kids love running down the banks.
📝 Anjou note
From Saumur west, the scenery becomes quieter and less touristy — more authentic villages, fewer crowds. The famous Anjou "douceur" (sweetness) is real and palpable.
🏕️ Camp🏟️ Roman amphitheatre
Day 13 · 13 Aug
🌊 Les Rosiers → Béhuard → Angers
~28 km
Stop at Béhuard — France's only commune situated on an island in the Loire. A tiny enchanting village a few hundred metres wide, with a 15th-century church built directly on a rock outcrop. Walk around in 20 minutes — magical. Then into Angers: one of the best cities in western France, with a massive black-and-white striped medieval fortress.
🏕️ Stay — BOOK NOW ⚡
Camping du Port Caroline — Accueil Vélo rated, 3-star, right on the EV6 route. From €7/pitch. Fills fast in August.
campingportcaroline.fr
🌊 Béhuard Island
France's only Loire island commune. Free to visit — cycle across the bridge. 20 minutes to walk around. The church built into the rock is extraordinary. Perfect mid-ride stop.
🏰 Château d'Angers
17 towers, 900m of walls. Inside: the Apocalypse Tapestry (100m long, 14th century). Adult ~€12 / under 18 EU free.
chateau-angers.fr
🏕️ Camp🏰 Giant fortress🌊 Island village⚡ Book Port Caroline
Day 14 · 14 Aug — Rest Day
🖼️ Angers — Fortress & tapestry day
0 km 😴
The Château d'Angers and its Apocalypse Tapestry deserve a full day. The tapestry is 100 metres long and dates from the 1370s — the scale genuinely stops children in their tracks. Afterwards: city parks, the Cointreau distillery tour (adults — Cointreau was invented here), or simply rest in the city. Consider upgrading to an AirBnb for this night — a washing machine and real beds before the final push to Nantes.
😴 Rest day🖼️ Apocalypse Tapestry🏠 AirBnb option🧺 Laundry & repack
4

Angers → Nantes

Days 15–22 · ~90 km · The Atlantic arrives. The Loire widens into something wild and majestic. Muscadet wine country, river swimming, a historic border crossing, and the most spectacular children's attraction in France as a grand finale.

🚲 6 riding days
😴 2 Nantes city nights
📍 5 overnight stops
~22 km avg
Day 15 · 15 Aug
🗺️ Angers → Ingrandes-sur-Loire
~25 km
The Loire begins to widen and feel more Atlantic. Easy cycling through meadows and quiet levée paths. Ingrandes was the historic border post between Anjou and Brittany — for centuries, salt and wine were taxed here as goods crossed between the two regions. Fun geography lesson: the kids can stand with one foot in "Anjou" and one in "Brittany."
🏕️ Stay
Camping Municipal, Ingrandes-sur-Loire — basic, cheap, directly on the river. Walk-up fine. ~€8–10/night. If full, continue 10 km to Saint-Florent-le-Vieil.
🍷 Muscadet Begins
You've entered Muscadet wine country — the Loire's great crisp white wine. Look for roadside dégustation signs. Most wineries welcome cyclists and offer free tastings in summer.
📝 Landscape shift
From Angers west the scenery shifts from château country to estuarine wild — increasingly dramatic. The river is noticeably wider and faster-moving. Brittany is near.
🏕️ Camp🗺️ Anjou/Brittany border🍷 Muscadet country
Day 16 · 16 Aug
🏊 Ingrandes → Saint-Florent → Ancenis
~22 km
A beautiful, easy wind-down stage. The Loire is wide and powerful here. Saint-Florent-le-Vieil has a remarkable clifftop abbey with panoramic river views — short detour worth taking. Ancenis is a charming small town with a ruined château and excellent supervised river swimming — a sandy beach on the Loire that's perfect for the kids all afternoon.
🏕️ Stay
Camping Municipal de l'Île Mouchet, Ancenis — on an island in the Loire. Shaded, quiet, excellent river access. ~€12/night.
+33 2 40 83 08 43
🏊 River Beach
Ancenis has a supervised river beach in July–August — lifeguard, sandbank, safe swimming area. Free. Perfect afternoon for all ages after a morning ride.
🕍 Saint-Florent Abbey
Clifftop Benedictine abbey above the Loire — the views are spectacular. Free to visit the exterior. Short 3 km detour off the main route.
🏕️ Camp (island!)🏊 River beach🍷 Winery visit
Day 17 · 17 Aug
🗼 Ancenis → Oudon → Nantes outskirts
~28 km
The penultimate riding day. Stop at Oudon — a beautifully preserved 14th-century octagonal tower above the Loire (climb for panoramic views, ~€4/adult). The path becomes increasingly shaded and lush approaching Nantes, following a towpath through willow groves. Last night under canvas — make it memorable. Tell the kids: tomorrow is the mechanical elephant.
🏕️ Final Camp Night
Camping de la Patissière, Barbechat or similar near Nantes suburbs. ~€15/night. Clean up thoroughly — you're checking into a hotel tomorrow.
🗼 Tour d'Oudon
14th-century octagonal tower, beautifully preserved. Climb to the top for panoramic Loire views. Adult ~€4 / child ~€2. Takes 30 minutes. Worth the brief detour.
🍷 Muscadet Tasting
This is the heart of Muscadet production. Many roadside wineries offer free dégustation. Ask the campsite to recommend the nearest one — great last-evening activity.
🏕️ Last camp night!🗼 Medieval tower
Days 18–21 · 18–21 Aug 🎉
🐘 NANTES — Grand Finale (3 nights)
~15 km into city
Ride into Nantes on 18 Aug via the shaded towpath. Cross the Tabarly bridge onto the Île de Nantes and head straight to Détours de Loire Nantes to drop off all bikes. Then: Les Machines de l'Île. A 12-metre mechanical elephant you can ride (carries 50 people), a three-storey Carousel of Sea Worlds, and workshops building new machines. Book tickets in advance — this sells out in August. The kids will talk about this for years. 19–20 Aug are full city days to explore, celebrate and rest, then fly to Porto on 21 Aug.
🏨 Stay (3 nights · 18–21 Aug)
Hotel or AirBnb, central Nantes — you've earned it! Aim for Île de Nantes or city centre. Budget €100–150/night. Book well ahead for August.
booking.com
🐘 Les Machines — BOOK NOW
Giant mechanical elephant (12m) + Carousel des Mondes Marins. Adult ~€26 / child ~€20. Sells out weeks ahead in August.
lesmachines-nantes.fr
🚲 Bike Drop-Off
