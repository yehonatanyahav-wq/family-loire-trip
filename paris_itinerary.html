Day-by-Day in Paris

A family-paced Paris — real magic without exhaustion. Key rule: one big thing per morning, then slow afternoons in a park or café.

Landing at Orly — Hour by Hour
Day 1 · 29 Jul · Arrival day
✈️ Your arrival (Transavia TO3691): You fly TLV → Larnaca on 28 Jul (Israir 6H581, landing 15:10), overnight in Cyprus, then Larnaca → Paris Orly on Wed 29 Jul, departing 12:45 and landing at Orly 16:25. That's a relaxed mid-afternoon arrival — you'll clear the airport and be settling into the apartment by early evening, with time for a gentle first walk before bed. The timeline below is built around the 16:25 landing.

16:25
Touch down at Paris Orly, Terminal 1–2–3
Transavia lands at ORY Terminal 3. Stay seated until the 4-year-old is ready — no rush, everyone else is also getting luggage.
16:45
Through passport control + baggage claim
EU/EEA passports use the e-gates (fast). Israeli passports go to the manned booths — allow 15–25 min if busy. Baggage belt typically takes 15–20 min. While waiting: one adult scouts the Metro 14 entrance signs.
17:10
🚇 Board Metro Line 14 at Aéroport d'Orly station
This is the big upgrade from 2025 — no more OrlyBus. Metro 14 departs from inside Terminal 1–2–3 (follow purple signs). Fully automatic, runs every 90 seconds. Buy tickets at the machine: €14/adult, €7/child (ages 4–9), under 4 free. Buy 5 tickets in one go. Contactless card works directly on the gates — easiest option.
17:35
Arrive Châtelet — transfer to Metro Line 5 direction Bobigny
Change at Châtelet (central Paris). Take Line 5 (orange) direction Bobigny and ride 3 stops to Bréguet-Sabin. Total underground time: ~35 min from Orly. The 4-year-old will probably fall asleep.
17:45
🏨 Check in — Citadines Bastille Marais
3-min walk from Bréguet-Sabin. Check-in is 24h, so no stress about time. Ask for a higher floor if available — quieter. Drop all bags, splash water on faces, change into clean clothes. You've made it.
18:05
🥐 First Paris mission: find a boulangerie
Walk 2 minutes to Rue Oberkampf or Bd Richard Lenoir — both have multiple boulangeries open into the evening. Order croissants, pain au chocolat, and a jambon-beurre for each child. This moment is the real arrival in France. With a 16:25 landing you'll have plenty of daylight and open shops.
Eve
🌿 10-minute walk: Place des Vosges for the sunset
If you have energy and it's still light (Paris stays light until 22:00 in summer), walk 8 minutes to Place des Vosges. Kids run on the grass. Adults sit under the arcades. Buy a bottle of wine from the Franprix on the way. This is Paris. No monuments, no queues — just the most beautiful square in the city, nearly empty at dusk.
21:00
🛏️ Kids in bed — early night for everyone
It's been a long day. The 4-year-old is running on fumes. Aim for 21:00 latest for the little one. Tomorrow is the Eiffel Tower — you need the early start.

2Full day
Eiffel Tower morning, Marais afternoon
Day 2 · 30 Jul · Your one full day
One full day, so make it the two essentials. Eiffel Tower early before the crowds, picnic and run-around on Champ de Mars, then an afternoon in the Marais — the most walkable, photogenic, family-friendly neighbourhood — with ice cream on Île Saint-Louis. Build in a midday rest back at the apartment if the 4-year-old needs it.
🗼 Eiffel Tower (morning): Be at the tower by 9am before crowds. 2nd floor is the family choice — better view than the summit, shorter queues. Book ahead at ticket.toureiffel.paris: ~€19/adult, ~€9 ages 4–11, under 4 free. Budget 2 hours.
🌿 Champ de Mars + picnic lunch: Huge lawn for the kids to run flat-out. Street-food vendors nearby. Then Métro across to the Marais (Line 6 → 1, ~25 min) — or apartment rest first.
🏛️ Place des Vosges (afternoon): Paris's oldest, most beautiful square — arcades for shade, central garden for the kids. Right by your Citadines base.
🍦 Île Saint-Louis + Berthillon: Cross the bridge for legendary ice cream and a stroll on the quiet island. Notre-Dame is a 10-min walk — see it from outside (restoration context for the older kids).

Train to Blois — Loire Begins
Day 3 · 31 Jul · Midday departure
Relaxed morning, croissants, check out and store bags if needed, then a midday direct train Paris Austerlitz → Blois-Chambord (~1h35), arriving early afternoon. Good direct options around 11:07→12:40 or 12:37→14:15. Night in Blois, then the cycle begins 1 Aug. Book seats ahead on SNCF Connect.
🚉 Station: Paris Austerlitz (Gare d'Austerlitz). Take Métro Line 5 or 10 to get there. Allow 30 min from the Bastille/Marais apartment.
🎫 Book on SNCF Connect: sncf-connect.com — 5 people with pre-booked seats is much calmer than turning up. Under-4s travel free; 4–11 child fare. Direct TER to Blois-Chambord, ~1h35.
🚲 Bike pickup: Détours de Loire in Blois. Call ahead to confirm cargo-bike / child-seat config and your arrival time. Cycle starts the next morning, 1 Aug.
🚂 ~1h35 to Blois 🎫 Book SNCF advance 🏨 Night in Blois
💡 Practical Paris Tips for 5 Get a Navigo Easy card (€2 chip) and load t+ tickets — each ticket is ~€2.15, kids under 4 ride free on public transport in Paris. A family of 5 will take lots of metro rides, so this adds up fast. Buy at any station ticket machine. For food, eat lunch (not dinner) in proper restaurants — same quality, half the price.

Best for the Kids

Paris rewards kids who are given time and space — not packed schedules. These are the genuinely magical spots.

🗼
Eiffel Tower
Second floor is the sweet spot — better views than the summit, shorter wait. The structure itself is the spectacle at age 4.
✓ All ages · Book online!
🎡
Jardin d'Acclimatation
Old-fashioned amusement park in the Bois de Boulogne. Pony rides, carousels, a little train, puppet theatre. Very French, very fun.
✓ Best for ages 3–10
🏰
Place des Vosges
Paris's oldest square — elegant arcades, a green garden in the middle, and a completely manageable scale for tired legs.
✓ All ages · Great picnic spot
🍦
Berthillon Ice Cream
On Île Saint-Louis. The queue is part of the experience. Flavours like salted caramel and wild strawberry. Worth every minute of waiting.
✓ All ages · Classic ritual
🌿
Luxembourg Gardens
The best park in Paris for kids — puppet theatre (Guignol), miniature sailboats on the pond, playgrounds, and wide sandpits. Bring a picnic.
✓ All ages · Free entry
🎨
Pompidou Centre
The outside is the attraction for kids — all the pipes and ducts painted in primary colours. The plaza has space to sit and watch street performers.
✓ All ages · Free to see exterior
🏨 Where to Stay with 5 People Look for aparthotels or family suites rather than standard hotel rooms — you can't fit 5 in a double. Citadines Apart'hotel, Residhome, and Adagio are solid chains with apartments. Best neighbourhoods: Marais (4th), Saint-Germain (6th/5th), Montparnasse (14th — close to Orly transport). Avoid Champs-Élysées area — overpriced. Île-de-France Airbnbs often give the most space for the price.
After Paris → The Loire begins Pick up bikes in Blois on 31 Jul and start pedalling west toward Nantes along the Loire à Vélo — ~380 km of châteaux, rivers, and camping over 20 days, then on to family in Porto.
