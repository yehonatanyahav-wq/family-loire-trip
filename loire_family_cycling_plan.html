🔑 Golden Rules for August with young kids
  • Ride early: Set off by 7am, aim to be at camp by 11am–noon. The Loire valley can reach 35°C by early afternoon.
  • The midday hours are for swimming, picnics, and château visits — not pedalling. Many châteaux have shaded grounds perfect for a slow afternoon.
  • The 4-year-old sets the pace. On a child seat, they'll nap as you ride — but they need regular stretch stops and will be tired by 10am on hot days.
  • Train escape hatch: The Train Vélo Loire runs Orléans ↔ Saint-Nazaire all summer with a dedicated bike carriage. If a day is impossible, hop on guilt-free. No booking needed on weekdays (€1 extra on weekends).
  • The route is entirely flat. The levées (raised river embankments) are the main surface — mostly compacted gravel and tarmac. No hills.
⚡ BOOK THESE NOW — August fills fast: Camping Île d'Offard (Saumur) · Camping du Port Caroline (Angers) · Any campsite in Amboise. All other municipal sites are generally walk-up fine, but these three are the most popular on the whole route.

Orléans → Blois

Days 1–6 · ~90 km total · The warm-up. Flat levée paths, the world's most famous château, and a magic show. Take it very easy — everyone needs to find their rhythm in the heat.

📍 5 stops
🚲 ~22 km avg
😴 2 rest days
🏕️ 4 camp + 1 hotel
Day 1 — Arrival
Orléans — Settle in & explore
0 km
Arrive by train from Paris Bercy (~2.5 hrs). Pick up bikes at Détours de Loire Orléans (58 Rue de la Charpenterie). Get fitted, briefed, and loaded. Spend the afternoon exploring Orléans gently on wheels — the Cathédrale Sainte-Croix, the Joan of Arc heritage, the old town. Evening: the Île Charlemagne has a water assault course for the kids (€5/hr). Camp just outside the city.
🏕️ Where to Stay
Camping de l'Île Charlemagne
On the island in the Loire, 5 min from city centre. Accueil Vélo rated.
~€15–20/pitch/night
campingfrance.com
🎯 Highlights
• Cathédrale Sainte-Croix
• Maison de Jeanne d'Arc
• Île Charlemagne water park
• Détours de Loire bike pickup
🍽️ Food & Supplies
Stock up at Orléans before leaving — next days are smaller towns. Get: sunscreen, insect repellent, water bottles, snacks. Market: Les Halles Châtelet (central).
🏕️ Camp 🏊 Water park 👧 Kids love it
Day 2
Orléans → Meung-sur-Loire
22 km
First proper riding day — keep it short and sweet. The path follows the south bank levée with beautiful wide river views. Stop in medieval Beaugency for ice cream and a look at the 11th-century bridge. Meung-sur-Loire is a quiet, deeply atmospheric village. The château here has a famous dungeon — kids love exploring it (used to imprison the poet Villon).
🏕️ Where to Stay
Camping Municipal de Meung-sur-Loire
Simple, shaded, very cheap (~€8–10/night). Right by the river. Walk-up fine in most years, but worth calling ahead in August.
Tel: +33 2 38 44 57 98
🏰 Château de Meung
Medieval bishop's palace with real dungeons. Kids go wild for the underground prison cells. Open daily in summer, ~€9 adult / €5 child.
chateau-de-meung.com
📝 Riding Tips
Depart by 7:30am. The levée path is mostly flat gravel — excellent surface. Beaugency is at km 15 — perfect lunch/ice-cream stop. Aim to arrive by 11am.
🏕️ Camp 🏰 Château dungeon 👧 Great for kids
Day 3 — Rest Day
Meung-sur-Loire — Slow day
0 km 😴
After travel + first riding days, everyone needs to decompress. This rest day is essential — don't skip it. A slow morning in the village, croissants from the boulangerie. Afternoon: river picnic, explore the castle at leisure, nap for the 4-year-old. Read, play cards, do nothing. This is the template for rest days — don't try to cram sightseeing in.
😴 Full rest 🥐 Boulangerie morning
Day 4
Meung → Muides-sur-Loire (Chambord area)
~20 km
Split the longer Blois leg over two days. This short stage brings you into the heart of the Sologne forest. Optional Chambord detour: the world's most famous château is just 8 km from the route — a stunning spectacle even from outside (and worth going in). The forest roads to Chambord are flat and completely car-free. Camp near Muides or Mer.
🏕️ Where to Stay
Camping de Bouquetins, Muides-sur-Loire
Lovely riverside site, 3-star, pool, good for families. ~€18–25/night.
Tel: +33 2 54 87 05 42
camping-les-bouquetins.com
🏰 Château de Chambord (optional)
World's largest Loire château. Even driving past is awe-inspiring. Entry: ~€15 adult / €10 child (under 18 free with EU residency). Vast grounds — free to cycle around.
chambord.org
📝 Note
If you do the Chambord detour (highly recommended), this becomes a 28 km day — still manageable if you leave by 7am and take a long lunch there in the shade of the grounds.
🏕️ Camp 🏰 Chambord option 👧 Jaw-dropping for kids
Day 5
Muides → Blois
~25 km
Easy morning ride into Blois. The city skyline emerges dramatically as you approach — the Royal Château dominates the hill above the Loire. Arrive by 10:30am, check in, then spend the afternoon exploring. Blois is one of the best cities on the route — treat to a hotel night so you can enjoy the old town in the evening without worrying about campsite curfews.
🏨 Where to Stay
Hôtel Mercure Blois Centre (cycling tour companies use this; secure bike storage, faces the Loire).
Or: Camping du Lac de Loire if you prefer to camp (~3km from centre).
Check: booking.com
🏰 Château Royal de Blois
Four different architectural styles in one building. Audio guide excellent for older kids. Don't miss the evening son-et-lumière show (light & sound, runs July–Aug, very child-friendly).
chateaudeblois.fr
🎯 Blois Must-Dos
• Maison de la Magie (magic museum — brilliant for children, adjacent to château)
• Old town streets & cathedral
• Evening meal at a riverside brasserie
🏨 Hotel night 🏰 Royal Château ✨ Magic museum
Day 6 — Rest Day
Blois — Full day in the city
0 km 😴
Don't rush out of Blois. Morning at the Maison de la Magie — one of the best children's attractions on the whole route. Lunch in the old town. Afternoon at leisure. Practical: laundry, restock, check bikes at the Détours de Loire Blois shop. Evening son-et-lumière at the château if you haven't seen it.

Blois → Tours

Days 7–12 · ~65 km · The golden stretch. Garden festivals, Leonardo da Vinci, river kayaking, and the best city on the route. Take two full nights in Tours.

📍 4 stops
🚲 ~22 km avg
😴 3 rest days
🏕️ 2 camp + 1 AirBnb
Day 7
Blois → Chaumont-sur-Loire
~20 km
A short, beautiful stage. The path hugs the river through poplar groves and quiet levées. Chaumont's château sits dramatically on a bluff above the Loire — but the real draw in summer is the International Garden Festival, one of France's most celebrated outdoor art events. Each year 30+ themed gardens are installed on the château grounds — a magical experience for all ages.
🏕️ Where to Stay
Camping de la Grande Tortue, Candé-sur-Beuvron
(~3 km from château, excellent family site, pool)
Tel: +33 2 54 44 15 20
la-grande-tortue.com
🌸 Festival International des Jardins
Runs Apr–Nov annually. ~30 contemporary themed gardens on the château grounds. Adult: ~€20 / child 6–15: ~€10 / under 6 free.
domaine-chaumont.fr
📝 Tip
This is a short day — leave Blois at 8am and you're there by 10am with the whole day free for the gardens. Perfect pacing for a hot August day.
🏕️ Camp 🌸 Garden Festival 👧 Magical for children
Day 8
Chaumont → Amboise
~23 km
One of the most beautiful stages on the entire route — the path winds through willow-lined levées with constant river views. Amboise is one of the true highlights. The Clos Lucé is Leonardo da Vinci's final home, with his workshop restored and life-size models of his inventions (flying machine, tank, helicopter) scattered through the gardens. The 8 and 11-year-old will spend hours here.
🏕️ Where to Stay
Camping de l'Île d'Or, Amboise
⚠️ BOOK AHEAD — most popular campsite on the route. On an island in the Loire, right in town. 3-star, excellent facilities.
Tel: +33 2 47 57 23 37
campingamboise.com
🎨 Clos Lucé — da Vinci's Home
Leonardo lived here 1516–1519. Life-size models of his inventions in the gardens. Adult: ~€18 / child 6–18: ~€12 / under 6 free. Allow 2–3 hours.
vinci-closluce.com
🏰 Château Royal d'Amboise
Dramatic hilltop château where Leonardo is buried. Good value at ~€15 adult / €10 child. The terrace views over the Loire are spectacular.
chateau-amboise.com
🏕️ Camp 🎨 da Vinci inventions 🚁 Flying machines! ⚡ Book NOW
Day 9 — Rest Day
Amboise — River day
0 km 😴
If you didn't fit both Clos Lucé and the Château yesterday, this is the day. Otherwise: the Loire has a sandy river beach just below the campsite — brilliant for young children. Kayak hire is available in town. The market on Friday/Sunday mornings is excellent. This is the trip's first real "resort" feeling — savour it.
😴 Rest day 🏊 River beach 🛶 Kayaking
Day 10
Amboise → Tours
~25 km
Follow the south bank through the famous Montlouis-sur-Loire vineyards — flat, quiet, beautiful. Tours is the biggest city on the route and deserves proper treatment. Get an AirBnb for two nights: washing machine, real kitchen, space to spread out. The old town (Vieille Ville) with its half-timbered houses and Place Plumereau is extraordinary. Best restaurant city on the route.
🏠 Where to Stay
AirBnb in Tours old town — aim for a place with a washing machine and 3+ bedrooms. Budget €100–150/night for a 2-night stay. Book well ahead for August.
airbnb.com
🚲 Bike Service
Détours de Loire Tours is right next to the train station — bring any issues here. They're the main depot and can fix most problems same-day.
37 Rue Charles Gille, Tours
🍽️ Tours Food
Tours is the gastronomy capital of the Loire. Must-try: rillettes de Tours (pork spread), Tarte Tatin, Vouvray white wine. Place Plumereau and Rue Colbert are the best restaurant streets.

Tours — City days

Day 12: optional day trip to Château de Villandry (30 min by train or taxi) — its Renaissance gardens are among the finest in France. Or simply rest, do laundry, and repack. Check bikes thoroughly before leaving.

aquarium-touraine.fr

chateauvillandry.fr

Tours → Saumur

Days 13–18 · ~80 km · Château country at its richest. Sleeping Beauty's castle, da Vinci's floating château, a clifftop medieval fortress, troglodyte cave villages, and an island campsite in the middle of the Loire.

📍 4 stops
🚲 ~27 km avg
😴 3 rest days
🏕️ 3 camp + Saumur base
Day 13
Tours → Villandry → Azay-le-Rideau
~28 km
Leave the main Loire and follow the Indre valley — quieter, more intimate, beautiful. Villandry is right on the route (if you didn't go on the rest day, this is your chance). Then Azay-le-Rideau: one of the most beautiful buildings in France, a Renaissance château that appears to float on the Indre river, reflected perfectly in the water. The 4-year-old will call it a princess castle.
🏕️ Where to Stay
Camping Municipal du Sabot, Azay-le-Rideau
Simple but well-located, 5 min walk from the château. ~€12/night.
Tel: +33 2 47 45 42 72
🏰 Château d'Azay-le-Rideau
Built 1518–1527, surrounded by a moat fed by the Indre. UNESCO World Heritage. Adult ~€13 / child free under 18 (EU). Summer evening light shows in the grounds.
azay-le-rideau.fr
📝 Route Note
This is a slightly longer day at 28 km. The Indre valley path is shadier and more sheltered than the main Loire levée — better for August heat. Leave by 7am to be at Villandry by 9am and Azay by noon.
🏕️ Camp 🏰 Floating château 👸 Princess castle!
Day 14
Azay → Château d'Ussé → Chinon
~25 km
Château d'Ussé is THE fairy-tale castle — it directly inspired Charles Perrault's Sleeping Beauty. Gleaming white towers, turrets, formal gardens. The 4 and 8-year-old will be absolutely speechless. Then Chinon: a magnificent medieval clifftop city with one of the largest château ruins in France. The campsite is directly on the Vienne river.
🏕️ Where to Stay
Camping de l'Île Auger, Chinon
On a river island in the Vienne. Excellent shaded pitches. ~€15–20/night.
Tel: +33 2 47 93 08 35
camping-chinon.com
🏰 Château d'Ussé
"The Sleeping Beauty Castle." Wax tableaux of the fairy tale inside. Peacocks in the gardens. Adult ~€17 / child 8–16 ~€11 / under 8 free.
chateaudusse.fr
🏰 Forteresse Royale de Chinon
Clifftop medieval fortress where Joan of Arc met the future Charles VII. Spectacular views. Adult ~€10 / child ~€6. The old town below is wonderful for an evening stroll.
forteressechinon.fr
🏕️ Camp 👸 Sleeping Beauty castle! 🏰 2 châteaux in 1 day
Day 15 — Rest Day
Chinon — Troglodytes & wine
0 km 😴
Chinon is rich enough for a full rest day. The troglodyte cave dwellings carved into the tufa cliff face are utterly unique — some are still lived in, many are open for visits. The Caves Plouzeau are worth a visit for the adults (wine cellar in a medieval cave). River swimming below the campsite for the kids in the afternoon.
😴 Rest day 🕳️ Cave dwellings 🏊 River swim 🍷 Chinon wine caves
Day 16
Chinon → Fontevraud → Saumur
~30 km
The longest stage of the trip — but packed with history. Abbaye Royale de Fontevraud is one of the largest medieval abbey complexes in Europe and contains the tombs of Eleanor of Aquitaine and Richard the Lionheart. Then Saumur: a beautiful château city with the most famous campsite on the whole route — on an island in the middle of the Loire.
🏕️ Where to Stay — BOOK NOW
Camping de l'Île d'Offard, Saumur ⚡
THE bucket-list campsite — on an island in the Loire, walking distance to the château. 3-star, pool, excellent. August books out months ahead.
Tel: +33 2 41 40 30 00
saumur-camping.com
⛪ Abbaye de Fontevraud
Tombs of the Plantagenet kings (Eleanor of Aquitaine, Richard the Lionheart). One of Europe's best-preserved medieval abbeys. Adult ~€12 / child ~€7.
fontevraud.fr
🏰 Château de Saumur
The iconic white château on a promontory above the Loire — the classic Loire Valley postcard view. Great museum of decorative arts inside. Adult ~€10 / child ~€6.
🏕️ Island camp ⚔️ Plantagenet tombs ⚡ Book Île d'Offard NOW
Days 17–18 — 2 Rest Days
Saumur — Island base
0 km 😴😴
Saumur is the best rest stop on the whole route. The Île d'Offard campsite is right on the Loire island — the kids can swim, you can sit and watch the river. Must-do: the mushroom caves (Les Caves Louis de Grenelle or La Cave de la Maroussière) — carved into tufa, fascinating underground world, completely cool in August heat. Sparkling Saumur wine tasting for the adults.
😴 Rest ×2 🍄 Mushroom caves 🏊 Loire island swimming 🥂 Saumur sparkling wine

Saumur → Angers

Days 19–22 · ~50 km · Gentle Anjou countryside. The Loire gets greener and wider. A Roman amphitheatre, a Loire island village, a medieval giant fortress, and the world's largest medieval tapestry.

📍 3 stops
🚲 ~25 km avg
😴 2 rest days
🏕️ 2 camp + AirBnb
Day 19
Saumur → Les Rosiers-sur-Loire
~25 km
A peaceful stage through lush Anjou countryside. Stop at Gennes — the small Roman amphitheatre here is free to enter and explore freely. Kids can run around the ancient tiers where gladiators once fought. The landscape gets visibly greener and more sheltered as you enter Anjou. Les Rosiers is a quiet riverside village with a good campsite.
🏕️ Where to Stay
Camping Val de Loire, Les Rosiers-sur-Loire
Quiet, family-friendly, on the Loire. ~€14/night.
Tel: +33 2 41 51 94 33
🏟️ Amphithéâtre de Gennes
1st–2nd century Roman amphitheatre, free entry. Not signposted on the main route — ask locally or use Google Maps. Kids love running down the ancient banks. 15 min detour worth taking.
📝 Anjou Note
From Saumur west, the scenery becomes more agricultural and less touristy. This is a good thing — quieter roads, more authentic villages, fewer crowds. The Anjou "douceur" (sweetness) is real.
🏕️ Camp 🏟️ Roman amphitheatre
Day 20
Les Rosiers → Béhuard → Angers
~28 km
Stop at Béhuard — a tiny, enchanting village that is France's only island commune in the Loire. A few hundred metres wide, accessed by a small bridge, with a 15th-century church built on a rock outcrop. Walk around in 20 minutes. Then into Angers: one of the best cities in western France, home to a massive black-and-white striped medieval fortress.
🏕️ Where to Stay — BOOK AHEAD
Camping du Port Caroline, Angers ⚡
Accueil Vélo rated, 3-star, right on the EV6 route, east of city centre. Pool, good facilities. Fills fast in August.
From €7/pitch or €69/cabin (4 people)
campingportcaroline.fr
🌊 Béhuard Island
The only Loire island commune. Free to visit — just cycle across the bridge. Walk around in 20 minutes. The little church built directly into the rock is extraordinary. A perfect mid-ride stop.
🏰 Château d'Angers
17 towers, 900m of walls — one of France's most impressive fortresses. Inside: the Apocalypse Tapestry (100m long, 14th century — the world's largest medieval tapestry). Adult ~€12 / child free under 18 (EU).
chateau-angers.fr
🏕️ Camp 🏰 Massive fortress 🌊 Island village ⚡ Book Port Caroline NOW
Days 21–22 — 2 Rest Days
Angers — City base
0 km 😴😴
Angers is big enough to absorb two full days. Day 21: the Château and Apocalypse Tapestry — genuinely one of the most impressive medieval artefacts in Europe (the scale stops children in their tracks). Day 22: city parks, the Cointreau distillery tour (adults), or an AirBnb night for a change of scenery and home cooking. Consider upgrading to AirBnb here for a washing machine and real beds before the final push to Nantes.

Angers → Nantes

Days 23–28+ · ~90 km · The Atlantic arrives. The Loire widens into something wild and majestic. Muscadet wine country, river swimming, a historic border crossing, and the most spectacular children's attraction in France as a grand finale.

📍 5 stops
🚲 ~22 km avg
😴 3 Nantes city nights
🏕️ 3 camp + hotel Nantes
Day 23
Angers → Ingrandes-sur-Loire
~25 km
The Loire begins to widen and feel more powerful. Gentle, easy cycling through meadows and quiet levée paths. Ingrandes was the historic border post between Anjou and Brittany — for centuries, salt and wine were taxed here. Fun geography moment: the kids can stand with one foot in "Anjou" and one in "Brittany." Good simple campsite here.
🏕️ Where to Stay
Camping Municipal, Ingrandes-sur-Loire
Basic, cheap, directly on the river. Walk-up fine. ~€8–10/night. If full, continue 10 km to Saint-Florent-le-Vieil.
📝 Route Note
From Angers to Nantes the route becomes progressively quieter and less visited. Fewer tourists, more locals. The landscape shifts from château country to estuarine wild — increasingly dramatic.
🍷 Muscadet Begins
You've entered Muscadet wine country — the Loire's great crisp white wine. Look for roadside signs for dégustation (tasting). Many wineries welcome cyclists and offer free tastings.
🏕️ Camp 🗺️ History border crossing 🍷 Muscadet country
Day 24
Ingrandes → Ancenis
~22 km
A beautiful, easy wind-down stage. The Loire is wide and dramatic here. Ancenis is a charming small town with a ruined château and excellent river swimming — a sandy bank on the Loire's edge that's perfect for the kids. The pace of the trip naturally slows here. Stop at a winery for a Muscadet tasting.
🏕️ Where to Stay
Camping Municipal de l'Île Mouchet, Ancenis
On an island in the Loire. Shaded, quiet, excellent river access. ~€12/night.
Tel: +33 2 40 83 08 43
🏊 River Swimming
Ancenis has a supervised river beach in July–August — safe swimming area, lifeguard, sandbank. Free. Perfect afternoon activity for all ages after a morning ride.
🍷 Winery Visit
Ask the campsite to recommend a nearby winery. Muscadet Coteaux de la Loire is produced right here. Most allow cyclists to visit without appointment in summer.
🏕️ Camp (island!) 🏊 River beach 🍷 Winery visit
Day 25
Ancenis → Oudon → Nantes outskirts
~30 km
The penultimate riding day. Stop at Oudon to see the extraordinary medieval tower on the cliff above the Loire — one of the best-preserved 14th century towers in France. Then the path becomes increasingly shaded and lush as you approach Nantes, following a towpath through willow groves. Last night under canvas — make it memorable.
🏕️ Last Camp Night
Camping de la Patissière, Barbechat (or similar near Nantes suburbs)
~€15/night. Clean up thoroughly here before checking into Nantes accommodation.
🗼 Tour d'Oudon
14th century octagonal tower, beautifully preserved. Climb to the top for panoramic Loire views. Adult ~€4 / child ~€2. Takes 30 minutes. Worth the small detour off the main path.
📝 Tomorrow Plan
Prepare the kids: tomorrow is the grand finale — the mechanical elephant in Nantes. Build the anticipation. It helps everyone push through the last 15 km into the city.
🏕️ Final camp night 🗼 Medieval tower
Days 26–28 🎉
NANTES — Grand Finale (3 nights)
~15 km into city
You made it. Ride into Nantes via the shaded towpath, cross the Tabarly bridge onto the Île de Nantes, and head straight to Détours de Loire Nantes to drop off the bikes. Then: Les Machines de l'Île. This is one of the most extraordinary children's attractions anywhere in Europe — a giant mechanical elephant you can ride, a three-story mechanical carousel of ocean creatures, and workshops building new machines. Allow a full day. The kids will talk about this for years.
🏨 Where to Stay
Hotel or AirBnb in central Nantes
You deserve a proper hotel after a month! Aim for the Île de Nantes or city centre. Budget €100–150/night. Book well ahead — Nantes is popular in August.
booking.com
🐘 Les Machines de l'Île
Book tickets in advance — sells out. The Grand Éléphant (12m tall mechanical elephant, rides 50 people) + Carrousel des Mondes Marins. Adult ~€26 / child ~€20.
lesmachines-nantes.fr
🚲 Bike Drop-Off
Détours de Loire Nantes
Drop off all bikes and equipment. They handle returning everything to Orléans. Get your deposit back. You're done!
Check their Nantes address when booking — they have partner locations in Nantes.

  • Camping de l'Île d'Or, Amboise — fills weeks ahead in August. campingamboise.com

🟡 Book 4–6 weeks before departure:
  • AirBnb or hotel in Tours (2 nights) and Nantes (3 nights)
  • Camping de la Grande Tortue (Chaumont-sur-Loire)
  • Hotel in Blois (1 night)
  • Camping de l'Île Auger (Chinon)
🟢 Walk-up / no booking needed: Most municipal campsites (Meung, Muides, Ingrandes, Ancenis, Les Rosiers, Gennes) are fine to arrive at without booking on weekdays. Have a backup plan for weekends.
