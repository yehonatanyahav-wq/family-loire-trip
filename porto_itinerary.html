~City
A day in Porto
Pick a cooler day · 23–26 Aug
One full day in the city itself — drive in (~35 min) or take the train from Esmoriz/Espinho. Porto is steep and walkable, with the Douro river at its heart. Easy to do the highlights at a family pace and be back at the villa by evening.
🌉Ribeira & Dom Luís I Bridge: The colourful riverfront and the double-deck iron bridge — walk the lower deck with kids. Big views, gentle pace.
📚Livraria Lello: The famous ornate bookshop (Harry-Potter-ish staircase). Timed tickets, gets very busy — book ahead or skip if the queues defeat you.
🚋Tram 1 along the Douro: Vintage tram from the centre to Foz (the river mouth). Kids love it; ends near beaches and ice cream.
🚂São Bento Station: Walls covered in blue azulejo tiles — a 10-minute wow on the way past.
🌉 Ribeira 📚 Lello 🚋 Tram to Foz
~Flex
Family time & coast — flexible days
23–26 Aug · Whatever you feel like
The rest of the week flexes around the family gathering and the weather. Some ideas within easy reach of Esmoriz:
🏖️Espinho: Lively beach town 10 min north — Saturday market, restaurants, more beaches.
🛶Barrinha de Esmoriz lagoon: Birdlife and calm water on the doorstep — gentle nature walk.
🍷Vila Nova de Gaia: Across the river from Porto — port wine cellars (kids can come; tastings for adults) and the cable car.
🏰Aveiro: ~30 min south — the "Portuguese Venice" with painted moliceiro boats on the canals. Good half-day.
